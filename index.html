Chocolate Chip Cookies

Yields about 20–24 cookies · Prep: 20 min · Bake: 10–12 min

Ingredients

  • 300 g chocolate chips
  • 250 g butter (room temperature)
  • 2 eggs (size M)
  • 100 g brown sugar
  • 130 g white sugar
  • 1 packet vanilla sugar
  • 330 g wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 pinch of salt
Tip: Replace 50 g of the chocolate chips with chopped dark chocolate for extra flavor.

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven: 180 °C top/bottom heat (160 °C fan). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar: Beat butter with brown, white, and vanilla sugar until light and fluffy (2–3 min).
  3. Add eggs: Mix in eggs one at a time until combined.
  4. Mix dry ingredients: Combine flour, baking soda, and salt. Add in two portions to the butter mixture, mixing just until dough forms.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips: Stir in chocolate chips evenly with a spatula.
  6. Shape cookies: Scoop dough balls (35–45 g) with a spoon or scoop, place on baking sheet with space, flatten slightly.
  7. Bake: 10–12 minutes until edges are golden and centers look soft. Do not overbake.
  8. Cool: Let cookies rest 5 minutes on sheet, then transfer to wire rack to cool completely.
For chewy cookies, chill dough balls 30–60 minutes before baking; for crispier cookies, bake immediately.

Serving & Storage

  • Serving: Best enjoyed warm with milk or coffee.
  • Storage: Airtight at room temperature for 3–4 days. Freeze raw dough balls or baked cookies up to 2 months.
  • Variation: Add 1 tsp cinnamon or 50 g chopped nuts for extra depth.
Happy baking!
